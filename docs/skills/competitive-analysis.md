# Competitive Analysis

Define who to benchmark and score them systematically. A competitive analysis is only as good as its frame.

## When to Use

- Starting a competitive benchmarking project
- Defining a competitor set before positioning decisions
- Preparing a market landscape for investors or stakeholders
- Validating differentiation claims

## Workflow

### Step 1: Establish Positioning Brief

Before scoping the set, understand the client's position:

1. **Identity** — What kind of company/product is this?
2. **Offer** — What does it deliver?
3. **Target customers** — Who does it sell to?
4. **Differentiator** — What's the moat or positioning argument?
5. **Strategic tension** — The paired axes that define white-space

**Do not proceed without the positioning brief.** A competitor list scoped without a lens is noise.

### Step 2: Identify Candidates

For each candidate, capture:
- Size and business model
- Niche / specialization overlap
- Geography and market reach
- Pricing and engagement model
- Technical depth / craft maturity
- Brand strength and distinctiveness

### Step 3: Tier the Set

| Tier | Definition | Typical Count |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **Direct** | Contests the same customers and positioning | 3-5 |
| **Adjacent** | Overlapping offer but different positioning or market | 3-5 |
| **Aspirational** | Where you want to be in 2-3 years | 1-3 |

### Step 4: Score & Benchmark

For each competitor in the Direct tier, score on:
1. Product completeness (features vs. your roadmap)
2. User experience quality
3. Pricing competitiveness
4. Market traction (users, revenue, funding)
5. Technical moat (proprietary tech, data advantage)
6. Brand/community strength

### Step 5: Synthesize

1. **2×2 positioning map** — plot competitors on two axes relevant to your strategy
2. **Gap analysis** — where are competitors weak that you can be strong?
3. **Threat assessment** — which competitors could move into your space?
4. **Recommendation** — positioning strategy based on the landscape

## Output Format

```markdown
# Competitive Analysis — {Product/Market}

## Positioning Brief
## Competitor Set (by tier)
## Scoring Matrix
## Positioning Map
## Gap Analysis
## Strategic Recommendations
```
