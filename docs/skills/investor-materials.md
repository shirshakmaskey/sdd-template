# Investor Materials

Build investor-facing materials that are consistent, credible, and easy to defend.

## When to Use

- Creating or revising a pitch deck
- Writing an investor memo or one-pager
- Building a financial model, milestone plan, or use-of-funds table
- Answering accelerator or incubator application questions
- Aligning multiple fundraising docs around one source of truth

## Golden Rule

**All investor materials must agree with each other.**

Create or confirm a single source of truth before writing:
- Traction metrics (users, revenue, growth rate)
- Pricing and revenue assumptions
- Raise size and instrument (SAFE, equity, convertible note)
- Use of funds breakdown
- Team bios and titles
- Milestones and timelines

**If conflicting numbers appear, stop and resolve them before drafting.**

## Core Workflow

1. **Inventory the canonical facts** — gather all current metrics and assumptions into one reference.
2. **Identify missing assumptions** — what numbers are needed but don't exist yet?
3. **Choose the asset type** — deck, memo, one-pager, model, or application.
4. **Draft the asset with explicit logic** — every number should trace back to an assumption.
5. **Cross-check every number** against the source of truth.
6. **Review for credibility** — would a skeptical investor find holes?

## Asset Templates

### Pitch Deck (10-12 slides)
1. Problem
2. Solution
3. Product (demo/screenshots)
4. Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM)
5. Business Model
6. Traction / Milestones
7. Competitive Landscape
8. Team
9. Financial Projections
10. The Ask (raise amount, use of funds)

### Investor Memo (2-3 pages)
- Investment thesis (why now, why us)
- Problem + market
- Solution + differentiation
- Traction + milestones
- Financial summary
- Team
- Terms

### Use of Funds Table
| Category | Allocation | % | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---|---------|
| Engineering | $X | X% | {specific deliverables} |
| Go-to-Market | $X | X% | {specific campaigns} |
| Operations | $X | X% | {specific needs} |
| Runway Buffer | $X | X% | 3-6 months buffer |

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- Never invent traction metrics or revenue figures.
- Never present projections without stating assumptions.
- Always include downside/risk scenarios, not just optimistic ones.
- Keep financial models simple — complex models with no data are fiction.
